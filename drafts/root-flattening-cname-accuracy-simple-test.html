Summary

I ran a test through App Synthetic Monitor about how IP resolution accuracy about plain cname and root flattening cname in CloudFlare. The CNAME needing to be resolved is a balancer hostname in PerfOps.

It seems to me that CNAME flattening on CloudFlare is far from accurate on IP resolution. Considering both of my IPs are behaving differently in every regions, I switched the site from root domain to www domain. Root domain looks pretty but on Cloudflare my best bet is subdomains like www when it comes to things like load balancers.

Maybe that's one of the reasons why gitlab redirects its root to about.gitlab.com. (But that's not the case for Github).

Anyway if I want accuracy while sticking to root domain, then I might have to switch to another dns service.

2019-06-18: My future go-to DNS service: NS1, Route 53. They both support GeoDNS, which solves what I need for root domain. The DNS of the domain has been changed to NS1. Now the domain just needs time for DNS propagation. They have a free plan with 50k query limits. Once I surpass that limit, I will consider switch to Route53 'cause it's much more cost-efficient.

At least the root Flattening is made for hostname resolved to a single host.

This test also proves that no such things like external cache affects the results too much.
